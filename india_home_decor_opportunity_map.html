5 validated market gaps
01 Renter-friendly, no-drill aesthetic decor
30M+ urban renters cannot drill walls. Zero brands have specifically built a "renter's decor" product range. Command strips exist but look cheap. Adhesive wall panels, peel-and-stick art, hanging systems with Indian aesthetics — this segment has no clear owner in India. Global signals from Etsy and Pinterest are massive. Price sensitivity: ₹500–₹2,500.
02 Curated "first home" bundles for newly married couples
India has ~9 million weddings/year. Every couple needs to furnish from scratch. No brand offers a cohesive ₹3,000–₹8,000 "start here" bundle — a curated set of 5–8 pieces that create an instant aesthetic. Pepperfry and Nestasia sell individual items; nobody sells a "complete vibe" at an accessible price. This is a massive gifting + self-purchase overlap opportunity.
03 Premium home decor gifting with Indian-modern design identity
The corporate and festival gifting market in India is ₹60,000+ Cr. Most gift hampers are predictable (dry fruits, generic candles). No D2C brand has owned "thoughtful, aesthetic, Indian-rooted home decor gifting" at the ₹1,500–₹4,500 price point. ITC entered scented candles in 2025 — the signal that this category is being taken seriously by large players, which means the window is now, not later.
04 Aesthetic WFH / desk-zone decor bundles
25M+ hybrid workers in India want a background that looks good on video calls. No brand offers a "WFH corner bundle" — a curated set of a small plant/succulent, a textured wall element, a desk lamp, and a ceramic piece that ship together as a kit. This is a precision opportunity: clear purchase trigger (new job, home office setup), Instagram-friendly content, no incumbent owning the space.
05 DIY textured wall panels — Indian aesthetic, accessible price
Textured walls are a top 2025 interior design trend in India. Currently only available through expensive interior designers (₹80,000+ per wall). No affordable DIY product exists. Peel-and-apply 3D wall panels with Indian motifs (jaali patterns, geometric, earthy tones) at ₹1,200–₹3,000 for a feature wall kit is a completely unclaimed product category. Viral potential on Reels is massive.

Supply & manufacturing

Fabric wall panels

Source block-print fabric from Jaipur/Bagru artisan clusters. Foam backing from Delhi NCR suppliers. ₹180–₹280 COGS per panel set. Sell at ₹799–₹1,499. Margin: 55–65%.

Curated bundles

Aggregate from 3–4 artisan suppliers (Rajasthan ceramics, Bengal textiles, Delhi brass). Assemble + brand in-house. COGS: ₹900–₹1,400. Sell: ₹2,499–₹3,999. Margin: 50–60%.

3D wall panels

Foam + resin panels from Noida/Gurgaon industrial suppliers. Custom mould for Indian motif needed (₹40K one-time). COGS: ₹350–₹500/pack. Sell: ₹1,499–₹2,999. Margin: 65–75%.

Sourcing risk map

Low risk

Textiles, ceramics, woven goods — massive artisan supplier base across Rajasthan, Gujarat, West Bengal. No single point of failure.

Medium risk

3D foam/resin panels need industrial supplier. 4–6 week lead time. Start with a single supplier and second-source in month 3.

Watch out

BIS norms on imported MDF (2025) tighten supply. Avoid products dependent on imported raw materials in year 1.

Why this over all other gaps

No incumbent

No brand in India has specifically positioned itself as "decor for renters." The segment is served by accident, not by design. First-mover advantage is real here.

Built-in virality

Before/after room transformation content is the most saved, shared format on Indian Instagram. Every product is a content brief waiting to happen.

Compounding TAM

30M+ urban renters today, growing with migration to metros. Each customer is recurring: they move every 1–2 years and re-decorate. High LTV.

Global playbook exists

Etsy sellers in the US/UK doing ₹2–₹5Cr/year equivalent selling "renter-friendly decor." India has zero equivalent. Copy, localize, win.
